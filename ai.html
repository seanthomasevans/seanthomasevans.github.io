The method

Four rules, every system

Rule one

Build the harness, not the one-off

When Premiere was too slow for ultra-slow-motion work on Daniel Lanois’ footage, the answer was not a faster edit. It was a PRD for what the system actually needed, an agent survey of every slow-motion method, a harness where clips land and render in several profiles unattended, and an eval loop that learns taste from the keep and kill calls. A hundred clips in, come back in two hours. The harness outlives the job.

Rule two

Validate objectively, gate with humans

Generation is cheap; trust is the product. Every take gets face-identity validation and a four-tier classification before a human ever sees it, and nothing ships on the system’s own opinion of its work. The taste call stays human, on an audit trail.

Rule three

When the tool fails beautifully, follow the failure

Slow the interpolation far enough and it runs out of information. It fails, but it fails in painterly ways. That glitch became the look of a release. Model limits are creative material, not just bugs to file.

Rule four

Make it defensible

Provenance ledgers on every generation. A five-level likeness-and-synthesis ladder with a per-model indemnity map for VFX work. Hash-chained audit logs for regulated casework. The deliverables are real: a Warner release, an Amazon series, a bank’s case files. The system carries the paperwork.

Regulated finance · TRIYO

An AML platform built with Triyo, the RegTech vendor, for its Libro Credit Union engagement. The standout piece is a crowd-simulation-inspired synthetic-data engine: agent-based actors carry legitimate spending rules, criminal actors carry a few extra, and fifteen laundering typologies emerge naturally inside a 1.2-million-transaction stream with automatic ground-truth labels. Around it: eight compliance agents, FINTRAC Gen 2 schemas parsed across 884 properties and 256 required fields, a hash-chained WORM audit ledger, and a deployed, password-gated AI-ops console on Azure.

Evaluation harnesses

The system never judges its own work

A multi-model judge scores copy, code, art, and audio across two frontier models in parallel. Ground-truth harnesses catch monotone voice clones by pitch variance against the real voice, and frozen or melted generated video by optical flow. The AML benchmark gates detector precision and recall against fixed thresholds in CI. Written methodology underneath all of it: falsifiable success first, objective validation, human gate last.

Local video pipeline

Fully local, HDR-finished

A self-contained video-generation pipeline pairing high-quality keyframes with open-weight motion models on a 96GB GPU, finishing to true HDR ProRes masters through a 32-bit EXR chain. Every clip is graded by an automated evaluation harness before a human pass, and the pipeline benchmarks its own fidelity gap against closed providers. Deploys the same stack to cloud GPUs on demand.
