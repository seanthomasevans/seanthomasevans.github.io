Broadcast production pipelines

The first database-driven production system I built was for Time Blazers in 2003, then rebuilt for its second season. From there I re-architected it from the ground up as a PHP and MySQL production-management platform for animators, directors, and shot review, and ran roughly 4,000 visual-effects and CG shots across 26 episodes through it. As renders outgrew the room, the pipeline moved to remote rendering with outside vendors, and then to AWS as soon as cloud rendering came online. Across film, visual effects, and broadcast deliverables through to the mid-2010s.

The first machine-learning work: solving temporal coherence for neural style transfer on the music video “Acid Test” for River Tiber, keeping a painted look stable frame to frame instead of boiling. Conformer

A production pipeline that compiles a screenplay into coverage shotlists and routes them to image-to-video models. A custom Fountain and Final Draft parser turns a script into scene, beat, and shot structure; coverage formulas are implemented as code; prompts compile across Flux, Hunyuan, Kling, World Labs, Veo, and Wan, with camera-motion mapping over seventeen motions and five models. Budget-gated dispatch, face-identity validation on every take, a JSONL provenance ledger, and assembly straight into DaVinci Resolve. Hardened on a feature at 774 shots and 2,558 takes, deployed on RTX PRO 6000 hardware. This is the broadcast shot-management idea, thirty years on, with generation in the loop.

The pipeline in use: a project carrying its script and shot canon, the agent that turns a screenplay into a shot punch list, and per-character reference canon that holds a face consistent across takes.

Local video pipeline

A fully local video-generation pipeline pairing high-quality keyframes with open-weight motion models (LTX, Wan, HunyuanVideo), finishing to true HDR ProRes masters through a 32-bit EXR chain. Its core is an evaluation harness that grades every clip on the principle that the system producing the work is never its own judge: optical flow catches frozen or melted output, pitch variance catches monotone voice clones, and clips are trimmed to their clean portion before a human pass. It also benchmarks its own fidelity gap against closed providers, and deploys the same stack to cloud GPUs on demand. Character and likeness work, with a five-level synthesis ladder and provenance ledger, runs here too.

A clean render that clears the evaluation gate, and a frame decomposed into PBR material passes

Sentra AML platform

An anti-money-laundering platform: eight compliance agents for transaction monitoring, sanctions and PEP screening, due diligence, and network analysis, on Azure with FINTRAC schemas parsed across 884 properties and a hash-chained audit ledger. At its core is a crowd-simulation-inspired synthetic-data engine: ordinary members carry legitimate spending rules and criminals carry a few more, so fifteen laundering patterns emerge inside a 1.2-million-transaction stream with automatic ground-truth labels. That labeled data scores fifteen behavioral detectors at 0.958 precision.

The public demo (Sentra): compliance dashboard, flagged activity, risk scoring, detection patterns

BirdNET field tool

A phone-to-desktop bird-sound identifier. A progressive web app records from the phone and posts audio and location to a FastAPI service running Cornell’s BirdNET model, reached privately over Tailscale. It returns the most likely species and enriches each detection with a written note and a reference image.
